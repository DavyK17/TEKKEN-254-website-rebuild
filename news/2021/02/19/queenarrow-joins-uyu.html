QueenArrow joins UYU

by TEKKEN 254

American eSports organisation UYU have announced Sylvia Gathoni, better known in the community as QueenArrow, as the newest member of their content team on their Twitter page. The team is widely recognised in the fighting game community, with some of the worldâ€™s top talent across multiple titles currently on their roster, including Tekken players LowHigh and Jeondding (South Korea), and YUYU (Japan).

QueenArrow, who primarily plays Lars, Xiaoyu, Leo and Asuka, became the first Kenyan Tekken player and the first female gamer in East Africa to be signed by an eSports team when she joined XiT Woundz (now XiT Gaming) in 2018. She later left the team to join defunct British outfit Brutal Democracy Esports in 2019, before leaving the following year to focus on her studies.
