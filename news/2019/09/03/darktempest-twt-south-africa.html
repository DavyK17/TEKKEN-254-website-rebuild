Darktempest to compete at Tekken World Tour event in South Africa

by TEKKEN 254

Nakuru-based player Darktempest is set to fly to South Africa to compete in Cape Town Showdown 7, the first Tekken World Tour event to be held on the African continent, which will take place on 7–8 September.

The Katarina main has been drawn in Pool 4, which also includes fellow Kenyan players Frost and Mickey, who both will be unable to attend the event for various reasons; DVK and QueenArrow will also miss the tournament, despite having registered to participate.

Unfortunately won't be able to make the @CTSFGC #TekkenWorldTour event in 2 weeks as I ran into some issues that would prevent me from getting an SA visa. Sad but confident things will go well for the other Kenyans…Africa needs to stand up and be counted at this event 🌍👊🏿

— Davy Kamanzi / DVK (@DavyK17) August 23, 2019

Unfortunately I won't be able to make it to the Cape Town Showdown come next week.😔 I'm really sorry about it...

— XiT | QueenArrow (@MalikaSiheme98) August 29, 2019

Darktempest first joined the TEKKEN 254 Circuit for Round Two of Season One in February 2018, finishing third in his round-robin pool and thus missing out on Top 8. He has since rapidly risen to become one of the Kenyan community's most formidable players, most recently qualifying for the Circuit's Season Three Finals in sixth place and being picked up by local esports team Simba Ultimate in the process. He put in one of his best tournament performances to date at the Finals, held at The Junction Mall on 3 August, coming in second amongst the Circuit's top 16 players throughout the year.

Everyone in the Kenyan Tekken community and larger FGC will be cheering Darktempest on as he tests himself against foreign competition offline for the first time. Cape Town Showdown 7 will be streamed live on the South African FGC Twitch channel from 12 to 9 pm SAST (1 to 10 pm EAT) on Saturday and from 1 to 8 pm SAST (2 to 9 pm EAT) on Sunday.
